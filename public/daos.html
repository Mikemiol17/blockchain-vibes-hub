A DAO is a blockchain-based organization governed by smart contracts and token holders. It operates without centralized leadership, allowing members to propose, vote on, and implement decisions collectively.

DAOs use governance tokens to distribute voting power. Proposals are submitted on platforms like Snapshot or Tally, and decisions are executed automatically via smart contracts once approved by the community.

  • Social clubs and communities (e.g., Friends With Benefits)
  • Media and content curation (e.g., Bankless DAO)

DAOs promote transparency, inclusivity, and decentralization. However, they face challenges like voter apathy, smart contract vulnerabilities, and legal ambiguity in traditional jurisdictions.
